Differential Privacy

Differential Privacy is a definition which can measure the privacy protection level. Based on it, some techniques is introduced to provide privacy protection for massive data publication which is working on dataset level. Some researchers also design mechanisms for a single user who is a participant in crowdsourcing to avoid the threat from untrusted crowdsourcers.

Definition

Every time we mention about differential privacy we have to show the definition:

The algorithm \(\mathcal{A}\) is \(\epsilon\)-differentially private if for all datasets \(D_{1}\) and \(D_{2}\) that differ on a single element (i.e., the data of one person), and all subsets \(S\) of \({\displaystyle {\textrm {im}}{\mathcal {A}}}\),
\[\frac{\Pr[{\mathcal {A}}(D_{1})\in S]}{\Pr[{\mathcal {A}}(D_{2})\in S]}\leq e^{\epsilon}\]where the probability is taken over the randomness used by the algorithm.
