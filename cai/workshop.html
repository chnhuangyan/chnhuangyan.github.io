Workshop of Socialized Congnitive Radio Networks

This workshop of Socialized Congnitive Radio Networks (CRNs)is hold in Georgia State University on February 22-24, 2019. In CRNs, two types of users, Primary Users (PUs) and Secondary Users (SUs), share common spectrum bands. SUs communicate through un-assigned spectrum bands without disrupting PUs. It is widely assumed that the activities of PUs follow some probabilistic models regardless of time, geography and social relationships. However, the time-and-geography-dependent social activity patterns of PUs can definitely be taken advantage of by SUs to obtain more spectrum opportunities and help with featuring the fundamental characteristics of CRNs in a more meaningful way. Therefore, this workshop will discuss the impact on both theoretical and practical aspects of CRNs as well as social networks. The topics include designing routing protocols/algorithms integrating technologies from social networks and traditional CRNs; designing statistic learning models and community detection methods considering PUs' activity patterns; investigating fundamental properties of secondary networks under certain activity patterns and community patterns of PUs; providing corresponding guidance for designing upper layer protocols for CRNs. New research challenges and significance of the corresponding problems will be discussed in the workshop.
